# Hi, I'm Vivek 👋

**Full Stack Engineer** at [FavorIt](https://favorit-website.vercel.app/) · MS CS, University of Florida

I'm a Full Stack Engineer at FavorIt, where I work across the entire stack with React Native, backend services, and payment infrastructure, building features that real users depend on every day.

Some of the things I've shipped recently: live trip updates using SwiftUI and ActivityKit, Stripe-integrated payment flows, and booking systems that have to hold up under concurrent load. I also built deep linking invite flows for iOS, which taught me a lot about how small UX details can have an outsized impact on growth. I care a lot about getting the details right, especially in systems where reliability actually matters to the user experience.

I have a Master's in CS from the University of Florida, where I studied Analysis of Algorithms, Advanced Data Structures, Database Management Systems, Natural Language Processing, and Software Engineering to name a few. While most of my theoretical CS knowledge comes from there, a lot of what I've practically learnt has come from working through problems in production.

---

### What I'm focused on

Right now I'm focused on going deeper in full stack engineering, distributed systems, and NLP and I believe in being a life long learner. I'm currently working through [Scrimba's Full Stack Engineer career path](https://scrimba.com/fullstack-path-c0fullstack), and the projects from that are on my GitHub here if you want to see what I've been building.

I also think a lot about how AI fits into the engineering workflow. I use Cursor, Claude, and Codex daily, not to replace the thinking, but to move faster through the parts that don't require it. Lately I've also been going deeper into how these models actually work like the context windows, what they can hold, how that shapes what you can and can't reliably ask them to do. The part I find genuinely interesting is figuring out where AI output is trustworthy and where it needs scrutiny. That judgment is still very much a human job.
